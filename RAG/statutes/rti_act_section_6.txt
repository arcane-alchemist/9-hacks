RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005

Section 6: Procedure for Seeking Information
(1) Any citizen may make a request in writing or through electronic means to a public information officer seeking any information.

(2) The request may be made in the official language of the state or in Hindi or in English. If the request is in any other language, the public authority may reject it but should inform the applicant to resubmit in the prescribed language.

(3) The request shall provide details of the information sought as specifically as possible. A blanket or too general request may be denied if the information cannot be reasonably identified and located.

(4) The request must be accompanied by the prescribed fee (as may be fixed by the state but not exceeding Rs. 100 for photocopies).

(5) A receipt of acknowledgment shall be issued by the public information officer indicating the date of receipt of the request.

(6) The public information officer shall provide the information within 30 days of the request. If the information pertains to a third party, the time period may be extended by 30 additional days to give the third party a chance to be heard.
