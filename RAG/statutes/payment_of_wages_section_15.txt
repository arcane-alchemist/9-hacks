PAYMENT OF WAGES ACT, 1936

Section 15: Penalties for Non-Payment and Late Payment
(1) Whoever contravenes the provisions related to payment of wages, minimum wages, or timely payment shall be punished with:
(a) imprisonment for a term which may extend to 6 months, or
(b) with fine which may extend to Rs. 500 (or higher as per state amendments), or
(c) with both imprisonment and fine.

(2) For subsequent offences within 3 years of the first conviction, the punishment shall be:
(a) imprisonment for a term which may extend to 1 year, or
(b) fine which may extend to Rs. 1,000 or higher, or
(c) with both.

(3) The employer shall also be liable to pay compensation to the affected worker equal to the wages withheld or paid late, along with interest at 12% per annum.

(4) The worker may lodge a complaint before the Inspector or directly approach the Magistrate under this section. The Magistrate shall hear the case and order payment within 30 days of conviction.
