SCHEDULED CASTES AND SCHEDULED TRIBES (PREVENTION OF ATROCITIES) ACT, 1989

Section 3: Punishment for Atrocities
(1) Whoever, not being a member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe, commits an offense against a member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe:
(a) imposes any disability on them,
(b) forces them to work in degrading conditions,
(c) denies them access to public services,
(d) insults due to caste,
(e) commits violence or sexual abuse, shall be punished with:
- Imprisonment for a term not less than 6 months but which may extend to 5 years,
- Fine which may extend to Rs. 5,000/- or both.

(2) If the offense involves sexual abuse or cruelty against a woman, the punishment shall be:
- Imprisonment for a term not less than 1 year but which may extend to 5 years,
- Fine which may extend to Rs. 10,000/-  or both.

(3) The offense is cognizable, non-bailable, and non-compoundable. Police shall arrest the accused without warrant.

(4) The victim may file an FIR at any police station or file a direct complaint before the Chief Judicial Magistrate.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
