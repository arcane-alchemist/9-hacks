PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005

Section 12: Protection Order
(1) The Magistrate may, after hearing the aggrieved person and after giving an opportunity of hearing to the respondent, issue a Protection Order requiring the respondent to:
(a) not to commit, attempt to commit or cause any act which would constitute domestic violence, or
(b) not to communicate with, or interact with the aggrieved person or any member of her family in any manner, whether directly or indirectly, or
(c) not to visit, come within or enter any area in which the aggrieved person lives or works or studies, or
(d) permit the aggrieved person to reside in the shared household with or without the respondent or any other members of the household.

(2) The Protection Order may be issued ex parte for a period not exceeding 30 days where the Magistrate is prima facie satisfied that there is an imminent danger of domestic violence.

(3) The respondent shall be bound by the Protection Order and failure to comply shall be punishable with imprisonment up to three months or fine up to Rs. 20,000/- or both.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
