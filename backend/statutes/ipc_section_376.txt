INDIAN PENAL CODE, 1860

Section 376: Punishment for Rape
(1) Whoever commits rape shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than seven years but which may be for life, and shall also be liable to fine.

(2) If the offense is committed by a person in authority or public servant, the punishment shall be rigorous imprisonment for a term of not less than ten years but may extend to life, with fine.

(3) If the rape results in the death of the victim or leaves her in a persistent vegetative state, the punishment shall be rigorous imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than 20 years but which may be for life.

(4) Rape is a cognizable offense and police can register an FIR without permission. It is also a non-bailable offense initially.

(5) The victim can file a complaint before the police or directly before a Magistrate. Medical examination and forensic evidence are crucial and must be conducted with the victim's consent at a government hospital.

(6) The victim has the right to legal representation throughout the investigation and trial, and may seek compensation from the criminal courts.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
