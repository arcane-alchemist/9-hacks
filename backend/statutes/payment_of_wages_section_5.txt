PAYMENT OF WAGES ACT, 1936

Section 5: Payment of Wages
(1) All wages shall be paid in cash or by cheque or by electronic modes by the employer to the worker. Where payment is made in cash, it shall be made in Indian currency only.

(2) Wages shall be paid directly to the worker without any deductions except as provided by law.

(3) No worker shall receive less than the minimum wages for the work performed.

(4) Wages shall be paid within the stipulated period, not exceeding 7 days from the date the worker performed the work, or as specified in the contract of employment.

(5) An abstract of the Act shall be posted in a conspicuous place in the establishment in the local language. This abstract must inform workers of their rights and the penalties for non-compliance by employers.

(6) Every employer shall maintain records showing the work done by each worker and wages paid to him. These records shall be available for inspection by authorized verifying officers.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
