PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005

Section 3: Definition of Domestic Violence
(1) For the purposes of this Act, domestic violence means any act, omission or commission or conduct of the respondent which:
(a) harms or injures the aggrieved person or threatens to harm or injure the aggrieved person, or
(b) injures or causes hurt to the aggrieved person or threatens to injure or cause hurt to the aggrieved person, or
(c) endangers the life, limb or health or safety of the aggrieved person, or threatens to endanger the life, limb or health or safety of the aggrieved person, or
(d) harasses, cruelty or causing cruelty to the aggrieved person by any conduct whatsoever, or
(e) attempts to commit any act or conducts any conduct which would constitute domestic violence, or
(f) causes physical abuse including unwanted or non-consensual sexual acts, or
(g) causes mental or emotional abuse including insults, ridicule, constant surveillance, deprivation of financial resources, denial of food, sleep or medical care, or
(h) causes economic abuse including controlling money, blocking access to property or assets, or
(i) causes psychological abuse including threats of harm, abandonment, confinement, or isolation from friends and family.

The aggrieved person shall be entitled to seek protection, relief and compensation from domestic violence as provided in this Act.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
