MINIMUM WAGES ACT, 1948

Section 3: Fixing of Minimum Wages
(1) The appropriate government shall, by notification, fix or revise the minimum rates of wages payable to workers employed in different scheduled employments.

(2) The minimum wage shall be fixed having regard to:
(a) the expenditure on food, fuel, light, housing, clothing, footwear and other necessities,
(b) the requirements of the worker and his family,
(c) the living standards prevalent among workers in the locality,
(d) the conditions of work and the hazards of employment.

(3) Separate minimum wages may be fixed for different areas, different industries, and different classes of workers (e.g., skilled, semi-skilled, unskilled).

(4) The minimum wage shall include basic pay and dearness allowance but exclude bonuses, commissions, and other allowances unless specified.

(5) Every worker employed in a scheduled employment shall be paid at least the minimum wage applicable to their category, location, and schedule.

(6) Failure to pay minimum wages is a cognizable offense and workers may file complaints before the Labor Inspector or the Magistrate.
