NATIONAL LEGAL SERVICES AUTHORITY (NALSA) - FREE LEGAL AID SCHEME

Overview:
The NALSA Free Legal Aid Scheme provides free legal aid to eligible citizens of India who cannot afford to pay for legal services. The scheme covers both criminal and civil cases.

Eligibility:
(1) Citizens with annual income below the prescribed limit (varies by state, typically Rs. 3-5 lakhs for individuals)
(2) Persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, women, children, and persons with disabilities
(3) Victims of human trafficking, bonded labor, and other vulnerable groups
(4) Industrial workers, farmers, and other economically disadvantaged persons
(5) Persons accused of criminal offences and seeking bail or legal defense

Services Provided:
(1) Free legal consultation and advice
(2) Free drafting of documents and applications
(3) Free representation in courts (civil and criminal)
(4) Help in filing FIR, RTI applications, and appeals
(5) Mediation and settlement assistance

How to Access:
(1) Visit the nearest District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) office during working hours
(2) Bring identity proof and income certificate
(3) File an application describing your legal issue
(4) A panel lawyer will be assigned to your case
(5) You can also call the NALSA helpline or visit NALSA websites for more information

Note: All services are completely free. No fees are charged for legal representation or advice.


Legal Note: These provisions are strictly enforced, and victims are encouraged to seek legal aid through local DLSA centers or legal clinics. Consulting a registered legal practitioner is advised for specific interpretations and filing procedures in court.

Important constitutional right: Under Article 39A of the Constitution of India, free legal aid is guaranteed to those who cannot afford it. Always seek professional legal counsel, and remember that justice through the courts is available to all.
